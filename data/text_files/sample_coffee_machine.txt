Coffee Machine Operations & Maintenance Guide
==============================================

This guide covers daily operation, cleaning, descaling, and troubleshooting
for commercial and home espresso machines. Use this file as a working demo
for the Vectorless RAG pipeline — replace it with your own documents.


DAILY CLEANING STEPS
--------------------

1. Portafilter and basket
   - Remove the portafilter after each shot.
   - Knock the spent puck into the knock box.
   - Rinse the portafilter and basket under hot running water.
   - Wipe dry with a clean cloth before re-inserting.

2. Group head
   - Perform a blind backflush using water only after every 10–15 shots.
   - At the end of the day, run a backflush with espresso machine cleaner
     (e.g. Cafiza) for 30 seconds, then rinse with three clean water cycles.
   - Use a group head brush to scrub the shower screen and dispersion plate.

3. Steam wand
   - Purge the steam wand for 2–3 seconds before steaming milk.
   - Immediately after steaming, wipe the wand with a damp cloth and purge again.
   - Soak the wand tip in a small container of hot water if milk has dried on it.

4. Drip tray
   - Empty and rinse the drip tray at the end of each day.
   - Wash with warm soapy water and dry completely before replacing.

5. Exterior surfaces
   - Wipe down the machine body, group head collar, and portafilter handle
     with a damp microfibre cloth.
   - Do not use abrasive cleaners on stainless steel surfaces.


WEEKLY MAINTENANCE
------------------

1. Deep backflush
   - Run a full backflush cycle with Cafiza or equivalent cleaner.
   - Follow with at least 5 clean water backflush cycles to remove all residue.

2. Shower screen removal
   - Remove the shower screen using a flathead screwdriver.
   - Soak in a solution of hot water and espresso cleaner for 20 minutes.
   - Scrub with a stiff brush, rinse thoroughly, and reattach.

3. Grinder cleaning
   - Use a grinder cleaning tablet (e.g. Grindz) once per week.
   - Follow with 2–3 small doses of coffee to purge any residual cleaner.
   - Wipe the hopper and grind chute with a dry cloth.

4. Water tank
   - If your machine uses a removable water tank, empty, rinse, and wipe it
     weekly to prevent mineral build-up and bacterial growth.

5. Portafilter basket inspection
   - Hold the basket up to the light to check for clogged holes.
   - Soak in hot water with cleaner if any holes are blocked.


DESCALING PROCEDURE
-------------------

Descale every 2–3 months, or when the machine's descale indicator lights up.

Required materials:
- Descaling solution (use the manufacturer's recommended brand)
- Large container or jug (minimum 1 litre capacity)
- Fresh water

Steps:
1. Empty the water tank completely.
2. Prepare the descaling solution according to the product instructions
   (typically 1 sachet or 30 ml of liquid per litre of water).
3. Pour the solution into the water tank.
4. Place the jug under the steam wand and group head.
5. Start the descale programme (refer to your machine's manual for the
   exact button sequence — usually hold the steam button for 5 seconds).
6. Allow the machine to run through the full descale cycle. This typically
   takes 20–30 minutes.
7. When the cycle completes, empty the jug.
8. Refill the tank with fresh water and run a full rinse cycle at least twice.
9. The machine is ready to use once the rinse cycles are complete.

Warning: Never use vinegar as a descaler in espresso machines — it can
damage internal seals and leave an odour that affects coffee flavour.


WATER TEMPERATURE SETTINGS
---------------------------

Optimal extraction temperature for espresso: 90–96°C (194–205°F)

- Light roasts: 94–96°C — higher temperature extracts more sweetness
  from lightly developed beans.
- Medium roasts: 92–94°C — balanced extraction.
- Dark roasts: 90–92°C — lower temperature prevents harsh bitterness.

To adjust temperature on most prosumer machines:
1. Enter the settings menu (typically by holding the programme button).
2. Navigate to "Brew Temperature" or "PID Setting".
3. Adjust in 1°C increments and save.
4. Allow the machine to reach temperature (2–3 minutes) before pulling
   a test shot.


GRIND SETTINGS
--------------

The grind is the most important variable in espresso. A correct grind
should produce a 30 ml double shot in 25–30 seconds.

Too coarse (fast shot, under 20 seconds):
- Adjust grinder one or two steps finer.
- Symptoms: thin, pale crema; watery flavour; sour taste.

Too fine (slow shot, over 35 seconds):
- Adjust grinder one or two steps coarser.
- Symptoms: thick, dark crema; bitter or astringent taste; choking.

Rule of thumb: change the grind setting by one step at a time. Always
pull a test shot after each adjustment before evaluating.

Grind freshness:
- Grind immediately before brewing for best results.
- Pre-ground coffee goes stale within 15–30 minutes once exposed to air.


TROUBLESHOOTING COMMON ISSUES
------------------------------

Problem: Machine is not producing steam
- Check the water level — refill if low.
- Ensure the steam boiler has had enough time to heat (3–5 minutes after
  switching on).
- The steam tip may be clogged — remove it, soak in hot water, and clear
  with a pin.

Problem: Coffee tastes burnt or bitter
- Temperature may be too high — reduce by 2°C.
- Check if the group head gasket is worn — a worn gasket causes channelling
  which leads to over-extraction on one side.
- Ensure backflushing is done regularly to remove coffee oil build-up.

Problem: Machine is leaking from the group head
- The group head gasket likely needs replacement.
- Gaskets typically last 12–18 months under daily use.
- Replacement is a 10-minute job: remove the shower screen, pry out the
  old gasket with a flathead screwdriver, press in the new one.

Problem: Low pressure / weak shots
- Check that the pump pressure is set to 9 bar (use a pressure gauge
  portafilter to verify).
- The pump may need servicing if it is more than 3–4 years old.
- Ensure the grind is not too coarse.

Problem: Machine is taking a long time to heat up
- Descale if not done recently — scale on the boiler reduces heating efficiency.
- If the machine is cold, allow 10–15 minutes for full temperature stability.

Problem: Grinder is making grinding noise but not producing grounds
- The burrs may be jammed with a hard object (stone in the coffee).
- Turn off the grinder, remove the hopper, and clear any obstruction.
- Never put your fingers near the burrs.


MILK STEAMING GUIDE
-------------------

For a smooth microfoam (flat white / latte):
1. Fill the jug to just below the spout.
2. Purge the steam wand for 2 seconds.
3. Insert the tip just below the milk surface at a slight angle.
4. Open the steam valve fully.
5. For the first 2–3 seconds, keep the tip near the surface to introduce
   air (the "stretching" phase — you should hear a slight paper-tearing sound).
6. Lower the jug so the tip is submerged and spin the milk in a whirlpool
   motion (the "texturing" phase).
7. Stop steaming when the jug becomes too hot to hold (approximately 65°C).
8. Tap the jug on the bench and swirl to integrate any large bubbles.
9. Pour immediately.

For a dry, stiff foam (cappuccino):
- Spend more time in the stretching phase — keep the tip at the surface
  for 5–8 seconds to incorporate more air.
- Target temperature is the same: 65°C.

Common milk steaming mistakes:
- Starting with warm milk: always use cold milk straight from the fridge.
- Steaming too slowly: use full steam pressure for best texture.
- Overheating past 70°C: milk proteins denature and the sweetness is lost.
